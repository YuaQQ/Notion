# Introduction

### PFAS & FAs:

- what they are
- current problem:
    - hard to decompose
    - waste treatment threatens environment
    

### Defluorination (F in PFAS → F- →  CaF2)

- Available techniques:
    - oxidation
    - UVC irradiation under extreme high temp & pressure
- Currently, mild-condition techniques are only available for breaking down smaller F-containing organic materials (e.g. PFOA: strong basic condition, 100 degree C).

### Gap addressed

- Now available
    - Mild-condition decomposition of PFOA
- Need: technique to break down PFAS under mild conditions.

### Research proposal

- Fact: LED-mediated nonlinear optical processes can photo-catalyze organic reactions.
    - Auger recombination: semiconductor nanocrystals can generate more and more excited electrons. → The excited electrons extracted and used?
    - Recent advancements in the nonlinear optical processes field
        - CdS/CdSe heteronanostructures
        - Mn-doped CdS NCs: allows energy transfer upconversion similar to Auger recombination
            - Limited to decomposition of allyl halide (potentially applicable to this research)
- Novelty
    - Applying the CdS & Cu-doped CdS NCs to the decomposition of PFOS & FPs, under room temp & atm.
        - Mechanism (Figure 1)
            - NC absorbs excited electrons, preventing their relaxation → electrons released into medium,  decomposing PFAS by reduction (Auger recombination)
            - In colloidal NC systems,  cooperative multiphoton processes promotes other ligands leaving the NC surface and thereby enhancing PFAS absorption onto the surface.
