# Introduction

- UWS, a common form of the disorders of consciousness (DoC)
    - absence of awareness
    - preserve hypothalamic & brain-stem autonomic functions
- Present ways to diagnose UWS
    - minimally conscious state (MCS)
    - locked-in syndrome (LIS)
- Novelty
    - examine consciousness in UWS patients through the neural synchrony of story comprehension of either a story of self / others from a familial / stranger to a UWS / healthy listener.
    - 4 hypotheses
        - Greater neural synchronization in healthy over UWS
        - Greater interbrain connectivity when listening to story of self over others (for healthy people) & no much difference for UWS patients (due to reduced hierarchal comprehension)
        - Greater connectivity between dyads of family members over strangers, for healthy people
        - Decreased difference between dyads of family members over strangers, for UWS patients

# Lit Review

- CRS-R has limitations in prognosis of DoC
- Cognition assessment using EEG, fMRI, fNIR
- EEG’s advantages
    - Easy to establish in clinic (compared to fMRI)
    - suited to functional sensory analyses requiring accurate timing of stimuli & responses
        - Semantic N400: shown to be effective in cognition assessment of sentence fragments in healthy & UWS people
- language comprehension in UWS may extend beyond simple language mechanics
    - E.g. patients’ name spoken by familiar voice (SON-FV) → auditory cortex activation significantly correlates with recovery
- Verbal communication hierarchies (results collected from EEG hyper-scanning)
    - interactive speech process
    - mutual understanding
    - relationship establishment & maintenance
- Previous SON-FV studies done by fMRI
    - Limitations
        - signal loss in cortices
        - fMRI requires independent facility
        - did not cover the effect of the relationship between speaker & listener on the patient’s brain activities
- EEG hyper-scanning
    - Current literature: focus on rhythm of speech → the third-level language comprehension in social context
    - Intensity of connectivity between listener & speaker: indicator of the listener’s level of understanding in the hierarchy of verbal communication, indicating the listener’s cognitive state
- This study
    - Using functional connectivity in EEG hyperscanning
    - Make the prognostic predictor of language comprehension
    - Evaluate the contextual comprehension in UWS patients
        - effect of social context (family member vs. stranger)
        - effect of language context (self / nonself stories)

# Results

- debiased weighted phase lag index (dwPLI): indicate functional connectivity
- 1st hypothesis: Greater neural synchronization in healthy over UWS
    - Figure 3 (nonself story; UWS vs. healthy)
    - Interpretation of signal intensity in figure caption, supporting the hypothesis
    - Text:
        - suggesting more significant neural coupling in healthy dyads
        - suggesting successful auditory stimuli perception in UWS patient & potential comprehension; but lower strength → simple understanding of the stories told
            - Other evidence supporting the claim
- 2nd hypothesis: Greater interbrain connectivity when listening to story of self over others (for healthy people) & no much difference for UWS patients (due to reduced hierarchal comprehension)
    - Figure 4: self vs. nonself stories; (UWS+healthy & familial+unfamilial speaker)
        - Interpretation of signal intensity in figure caption
            - Great intrabrain connectivity for nonself stories in speaker
            - Great intrabrain connectivity for self story in listener
            - Weaker interbrain connectivity
    - Figure 5: self vs. nonself stories; UWS vs. healthy
        - Text: only slight differences in neural connectivity between self & nonself stories, both in UWS & healthy
        - Interpretation: the content & context of the narrative has no impact on neural synchronization regardless of the cognition state in the listener.
- 3rd hypothesis: Greater connectivity between dyads of family members over strangers, for healthy people
    - Figure 6: self vs. nonself stories; told by stranger vs. family member (UWS + healthy combined)
        - stronger intra- and interbrain connectivity when nonself story (over self) is read by family members.
- To further examine the possibility of using the difference as a prognostic predictor
    - Figure 7: self story; told by stranger vs. family member; UWS vs. healthy
    - Figure 8: nonself story; told by stranger vs. family member; UWS vs. healthy
        - UWS: stronger interbrain connectivity in response to family members than stranger
        - healthy: stronger interbrain connectivity in response to stranger than family member
        - Family member: no difference between healthy & UWS listeners
