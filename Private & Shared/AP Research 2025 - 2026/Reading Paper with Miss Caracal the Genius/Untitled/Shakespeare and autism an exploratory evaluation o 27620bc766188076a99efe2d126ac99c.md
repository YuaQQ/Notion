# Introduction

---

- ASD patients have deficits in pragmatic language
- Traditional interventions to teach social skills. Limitations include:
    - Relying on explicit instruction
    - predictable organization to minimize stress
    - too highly structured to teach social-emotional & play skills
- What can socio-dramatic play d to help kids understand the perspectives of others, practice communication & understand social norms
    
    
    ## Drama-based intervention
    
    - What can drama-based interventions do (citations)
        
        ### Detailed lit review (what is done in each study + results)
        
        - 2013 study: improvement in observed social behavior in natural setting compared to a clinical control group
        - 2011 pilot study: Social Emotional NeuroScience Endocrinology Theatre intervention improved in face identification & theory of mind skills
        - 2014 study: NEPSY, Parenting Stress Index, Adaptive Behavior Assessment System, Social Responsiveness Scale etc. improves face processing, social awareness, and social cognition.
        - 2016 study: impact of Social Emotional NeuroScience Endocrinology Theatre intervention on anxiety & stress; results indicate improvements in social competence & in trait anxiety

## Hunter-Heartbeat Method

- What is it (how it is conducted, what it may achieve)
- Advantage
    - Intrinsic reinforcement due to playful & humorous nature of games
    - Allowing ASD kids to learn social skills in an ecologically valid environment
- Differences compared to other drama-based interventions
    - Drama based games; not culminating into a full production or performance
    - Unlike social-dramatic interventions (also using game-based approach), string together following a storyline
    - Unlike Social Emotional NeuroScience Endocrinology Theatre intervention, kids can play multiple social roles
    - Relies on the work of Shakespeare to create the social & emotional contexts for practicing social skills & exploring various narrative, emotional, & behavioral choices in the context of social-dramatic play.

## Aim

First systematic implementation of Heartbeat Method to treat for social deficits associated with ASD.

# Discussion

---

- Novelty
    - The first systematic implementation of the Heartbeat Method (Previously: informal implementation)
    - Training of the facilitators
    - Choice of participants and optimal timing of intervention
    - Social validity questionnaires
        - Parent quotes from questionnaires as evidence
    - Outcome measures
        - Vinland-II & Test of Pragmatic Language-2 were sensitive to change in skill across 10 weeks of treatment
        - Penn Facial Recognition Facial Emotion Recognition Task was not sensitive enough
            - Reasoning
    - Limitations
        - Results limited to the characteristics of the sample & may not be generalizable to older or younger SD kids / kids with more limited language skills
        - Small sample size
        - No control group
    - Future directions
