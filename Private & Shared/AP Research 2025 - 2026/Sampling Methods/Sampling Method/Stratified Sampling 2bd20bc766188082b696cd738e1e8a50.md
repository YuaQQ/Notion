# Stratified Sampling

Core Principle: 1st step: Stratify.
2nd step: Sample randomly within strata
Primary Applicable Scenarios: Clearly stratified population (subgroup representativeness should be ensured)
Type: Probability

The findings from the sample need to be generalized to the population, aiming for representativeness and objectivity. Allows for statistical inference.

# Procedure

1. The population is divided into different strata (groups) based on an important characteristic (e.g. gender, age, region, class), ensuring high homogeneity within strata and high heterogeneity between strata. 
2. Independent random samples are drawn from each stratum.

# Advantages

- Ensures the sample's structure matches the population on key characteristics
- Improves estimation precision
- Facilitates comparisons between strata

# Scenario

The population has clear, relevant categories related to the research topic.

# Example

Studying university students' spending habits by first stratifying by year of study, then randomly sampling within each year.
