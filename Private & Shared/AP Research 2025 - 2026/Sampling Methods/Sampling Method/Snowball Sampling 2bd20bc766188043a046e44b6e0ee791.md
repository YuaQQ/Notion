# Snowball Sampling

Core Principle: Obtain sample through referral chains
Primary Applicable Scenarios: Research on hard-to-reach, hidden specific populations
Type: Non-Probability

Suitable for exploratory research, qualitative research, pilot testing, or when probability sampling is not feasible. The focus is on deep understanding rather than generalizability.

# Procedure

1. Identify & interview a few initial respondents who meet the eligibility criteria
2. Ask the initial respondents to recommend other potentially qualified respondents
3. The process continues, gradually increasing the sample size just like a rolling snowball

# Scenario

- Hidden, or hard-to-reach populations
    - Used when there is no clear sampling frame (list) for the target population, or when members have sensitive or private identities (e.g., HIV patients, sex workers, specific subcultures, high-level professionals, undocumented immigrants)
- Used in qualitative research, or the early stages of exploratory studies

# Advantages & Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Effectively reaching populations inaccessible through ordinary methods.
- Low cost; relatively simple to implement.

### Disadvantages

- Poor representativeness due to sampling heavily relying on social networks
    - cliques
    - high homogeneity
    - fail to reflect the diversity of the population
- High selection bias: individuals with better social connections are more likely to be included
- Not suitable for quantitative research requiring statistical inference
