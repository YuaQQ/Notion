# Simple Random Sampling

Core Principle: Equal probability, purely random
Primary Applicable Scenarios: Fundamental method; applicable in small population with an easily obtainable list
Type: Probability

The findings from the sample need to be generalized to the population, aiming for representativeness and objectivity. Allows for statistical inference.

# Procedure

Individuals are selected completely randomly from the population, with each having an equal probability of selection.

# Scenario

The population is small and units can be easily numbered. It forms the basis for more complex sampling methods. Example: Randomly selecting 100 employees from a list of 1000 for a satisfaction survey.
