# Quota Sampling

Core Principle: Fill quotas set to match population structure
Primary Applicable Scenarios: Market research, opinion polls where structurally approximate samples are obtained
Type: Non-Probability

Suitable for exploratory research, qualitative research, pilot testing, or when probability sampling is not feasible. The focus is on deep understanding rather than generalizability.

# Procedure

1. Similar to stratified sampling, quotas are first set based on the population's composition (e.g. gender ratio, age ratio)
2. Then, the interviewers **non-randomly** find respondents to fill these quotas

# Scenario

Market research, opinion polls when probability sampling is impossible but a sample roughly matching the population structure on key characteristics is desired. It improves the structure of convenience sampling, but the right to determine which ones to choose remains with the interviewer.
