# Classification by Response Format

## Structured 结构式

---

Often referred to as **closed-ended** or **fixed-response** questionnaires. In this format, the researcher pre-determines the response options, and the respondent selects the appropriate answer by circling or checking a box.

## Open-Ended 开放式

---

Also known as **free-response** questionnaires. This type does not provide fixed answers, allowing respondents to articulate their thoughts freely.

## Semi-Structured 半结构式

This format is an intermediate blend of the structured and open-ended types. The questions include both fixed, standard response options and space for respondents to elaborate freely, thus incorporating the advantages of both types. This format is widely utilized in practical survey research.

# Classification by Administration Method

## Interviewer-administered Questionnaire 访问问卷

---

Typically yield the **highest response rate** and the **most reliable results**, but they are **costly** and **time-consuming**. A response rate of over 90% is generally required for this type.

## Self-administered Questionnaire 自填问卷

---

- **Mailed Self-administered** **Questionnaire 邮寄自填问卷**: sent by the research organization to the respondent, who completes it and mails it back to the organization.
    - Have a **low response rate**, and the survey process cannot be controlled, leading to lower credibility and validity. The low response rate can also cause **sampling bias**, affecting the accuracy of the results. Generally, a response rate of around 50% is considered acceptable for mailed surveys.
    
- **Delivered Self-administered Questionnaire 发送式自填问卷**: directly handed to the respondent by the investigator and collected directly by the same investigator
    - Possess advantages and disadvantages that fall between the two above-mentioned types, requiring a response rate of over 67%.

# Classification by Purpose

## Screening Questionnaire 甄别问卷

---

Designed to ensure that the respondents are indeed the target consumer group for the product being studied. The questions typically cover five areas of exclusion: **socio-demographic variables** (age, gender, education, income, etc.), **product applicability**, **product usage frequency**, **states with special influence on product evaluation**, and **exclusion of survey refusers**.

## Main Survey Questionnaire 调查问卷

---

This is the most essential component of the survey and constitutes the core of the research. Any survey can exist without a screening or validation questionnaire, but it **must** include a main survey questionnaire, as it forms the basis for analysis.

## Validation Questionnaire 回访问卷

---

Also known as a **callback** or **verification questionnaire**, this is a supervisory tool designed to check whether interviewers adhered to the required interviewing procedures. It typically comprises the introduction, all questions from the screening questionnaire, and a selection of key questions from the main survey questionnaire.
