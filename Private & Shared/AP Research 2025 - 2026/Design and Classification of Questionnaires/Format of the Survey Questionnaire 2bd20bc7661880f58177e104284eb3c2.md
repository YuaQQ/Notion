# Format of the Survey Questionnaire

[Source Page](https://zhuanlan.zhihu.com/p/525566568)

### 1. Questionnaire Title

The title should concisely summarize the research topic, providing respondents with a general understanding of the questions they are about to answer. The title must be **brief and to the point**, while still clearly indicating the research subject or theme. For example, use **"Survey on the Current Status of Hot Water Supply in Student Dormitory Bathrooms"** rather than the overly simplistic **"Hot Water Problem Survey Questionnaire."** The latter fails to clearly inform the respondent of the subject matter, which can hinder their thought process when answering the questions.

### 2. Questionnaire Instructions (Introduction)

A brief introduction is typically placed at the beginning of the questionnaire. Its primary function is to explain the **significance** of the research, the **content** covered, and the **method for selecting answers**, thereby alleviating any respondent anxiety or apprehension. The instructions should be **concise and to the point**, using language that is cordial but not overly informal.

### 3. Completion Guidelines

For self-administered questionnaires, instructions on how to complete the form should be provided. These guidelines can be integrated into the main instructions or listed separately. A separate listing is advantageous as it makes the requirements clearer and is more likely to capture the respondent's attention.

### 4. Main Subject Questions (The Core)

This section lists the research questions step-by-step, following the survey design. It is the **main body** of the questionnaire, and its quality directly determines the overall value of the research.

### 5. Coding

Coding is the process of converting the survey items and selected answers into a uniform, pre-designed set of codes. Coding all questionnaires facilitates **computer processing and statistical analysis**. A numerical code system is generally used, and a dedicated space for **"Statistical Code"** is typically reserved on the far right side of the questionnaire.

### 6. Respondent's Demographic Information

This refers to the respondent's key characteristics, such as **name, gender, age, ethnicity, place of origin, and affiliated department/school**. These serve as the basic control variables for categorized analysis. The specific information requested should be chosen based on the context of the research; asking for too much information is not advisable. If the respondent's characteristics are not required for the statistical analysis of the responses, these questions should be omitted. These questions are generally best placed at the **end** of the questionnaire. If they do not involve sensitive personal information, they can alternatively be placed after the **Questionnaire Instructions**.

### 7. Interviewer Information (if applicable)

At the end of the questionnaire, the **name of the interviewer**, the **date of the interview**, and the **start and end times** should be included. This information is crucial for monitoring and controlling the quality of the data collection. If the respondent's demographic information is placed after the main instructions, the interviewer's information can also be integrated into the same section or table.

### 8. Concluding Remarks (Closing)

The conclusion usually employs one of three expressive formats:

- **Comprehensive:** This format expresses renewed **gratitude** for the respondent's cooperation and includes a request to **check for any missed or incorrect answers**. This demonstrates consistent politeness and encourages the respondent to complete any unanswered items and correct any errors.
- **Open-ended:** This involves introducing a significant, final question to understand the respondent's opinions or thoughts that could not be captured by the standardized questions. This **open-ended question is strategically placed at the very end**.
- **Responsive:** This format seeks the respondent's **feedback** or **opinions** regarding the format or content of the survey itself. The questions can be either closed-ended or open-ended.
